Model Development:  

The Decision Tree Classifier model was developed and fine-tuned using the dataset, which was divided into training and testing sets. The training set allowed the model to recognize patterns, while the testing set evaluated its ability to generalize to new data. This method mitigates overfitting, ensuring the model performs effectively not just on known data but also on previously unseen cases. Utilizing a test set helps maintain unbiased and reliable predictions.





Accuracy is 100%
 Accuracy is the ratio of correctly predicted observations to the total observations.
Accuracy=(True Positives + True Negatives) / Total Samples

F1 Score is 100%
The F1 score is the harmonic mean of Precision and Recall.  It shows the perfect  balance between Precision and Recall.
F1= 2*((Precision* Recall) /(Precision + Recall))
Precision is 100%
Precision (also called Positive Predictive Value) is the ratio of correctly predicted positive observations to the total predicted positive observations. It doesn't give a false positive.
Precision=(True Positive/(True Positive + False Positive))
Recall is 100%
Recall (also called Sensitivity or True Positive Rate) is the ratio of correctly predicted positive observations to all actual positive observations. It doesn't miss a case, so no false negative.
Recall=(True Positive/ (True Positive + False Negative))
