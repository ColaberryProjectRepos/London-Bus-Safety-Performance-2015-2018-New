Lesson learned and Future Work 9


Developing the Power BI dashboard for TFL bus safety performance analysis provided several valuable lessons, particularly in data preparation, analysis, visualization, and decision-making.
  • Data Cleaning is Critical: The dataset required handling missing values, standardizing formats, and correcting inconsistencies to ensure accurate analysis. This step reinforced the importance of data preprocessing in delivering reliable insights.
  • Understanding Data Relationships: Identifying key dependent and independent variables in building meaningful visualizations and extracting trends.
  • Effective KPI Selection Enhances Decision-Making: Choosing the right KPIs (Total Incidents, Fatal Rate %, Injury Rate %, Hospitalization Rate %) ensured that the report focused on the most impactful safety metrics, providing clarity to stakeholders.
  • Importance of Year-over-Year Trend Analysis: Tracking incident trends over multiple years helped reveal the effectiveness of safety measures and allowed for strategic adjustments in interventions.
  • Customization and Interactivity Matter: Incorporating filters for boroughs and years allowed users to drill down into specific data points, making the dashboard more intuitive and user-friendly.
  • Visualization Drives Clarity: The use of stacked column charts, line graphs, and comparative analysis by Group Names and Operators, Map visualization to show the number of incidents, categorized by incident type descriptions with respect to Boroughs, helped in better understanding safety performance variations across different service providers.



    Future Capabilities: 


    • Real-Time Data Integration: Connect with onboard sensors, CCTV footage, and AI-based incident detection systems for live monitoring.
    • IoT (Internet of Things Technology)-Enabled Tracking: Use GPS and smart sensors to track hazardous driving behaviors, congestion hotspots, and route disruptions.
    • Predictive Analytics with Machine Learning: Analyze historical data to identify routes with a high probability of future incidents.
    • Automated Safety Interventions: Adjust bus speeds based on real-time weather or deploy safety personnel to high-risk areas.
    • Geospatial Mapping & Clustering Analysis: Identify borough-specific safety concerns through advanced data visualization.
    • Crowdsourced Passenger Feedback: Allow commuters to report incidents in real-time, improving response times and safety measures.
    • Automated Safety Compliance Checks: Ensure operators follow training programs and vehicle maintenance schedules.
    • Scalable Urban Transit Model: Create a data-driven safety system that can be replicated in other global cities.
