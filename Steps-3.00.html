Methodology 3

Data Preparation:  

The data cleaning process included addressing missing values, standardizing data formats, eliminating duplicates, and resolving inconsistencies. These steps ensured the dataset was well-structured, refined, and optimized for accurate predictive modeling and effective visualization.




Model Selection: 

A classification-based approach can be highly effective in analyzing boroughs with a higher number of incidents based on incident result descriptions. By using machine learning classification models such as Decision Tree, Random Forest, or Logistic Regression, the boroughs can be categorized based on the severity of incidents (e.g., minor injury, serious injury, hospitalization, or fatality).  The independent variables (borough, route, operator, group name, incident type, and victim details) influence the dependent variable (incident result description) by determining the likelihood of different injury severities. Certain boroughs, operators, or routes might show a higher correlation with severe injuries due to factors like traffic conditions, driver training, or infrastructure limitations. By analyzing these relationships, authorities can identify patterns and implement safety-focused interventions  on high-risk areas , high-risk operators and service providers.
