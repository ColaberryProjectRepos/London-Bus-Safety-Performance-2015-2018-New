Data Overview 2

The bus safety incidents data submitted to TFL (Transportation for London) by it's contracted bus operators. Operators report directly into pan-London system to help TFL  monitor overall trends in safety performance. This data set includes all the related parameters to incidents and injuries. It covers incidents from 2015 to 2018.

System Architecture:

The data analysis process for the bus incident dataset begins with data collection and preprocessing, ensuring consistency by handling missing values, standardizing formats, and removing duplicates, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) follows, using statistical summaries and visualizations  to identify trends in total incidents, fatality rates, and hospitalization percentages across boroughs and operators. The insights drive Power BI dashboard development, incorporating KPIs, year-over-year trends, and comparative case studies (e.g., Westminster vs. Southwark) to assess safety measures' effectiveness. The final step involves recommendations and decision support, proposing strategies like improved driver training, AI-driven risk detection, and real-time safety monitoring to enhance passenger safety and urban mobility.




Data Features:

The bus incident dataset consists of multiple features that provide insights into accident trends, safety risks, and operator performance across London. Temporal features include the year and date of the incident, while geographic features cover the borough and bus garage location. Operational details such as route number, operator, and group name help track service providers involved in incidents. Incident-specific attributes like event type (collision, assault, vandalism), injury severity (minor, serious, fatal), and victim details (category, sex, and age group) offer a deeper understanding of accident impact. These features enable clustering of high-risk routes, trend analysis, and the development of safety strategies to enhance urban mobility and reduce injuries.
